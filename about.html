About SMART

Seeking Modern Applications for Real Transformation (SMART) was registered in 1997 under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 as a non-profit organisation. Since its inception, SMART has worked with vulnerable and marginalised communities, with a strong focus on women’s rights, gender equality, and democratic participation.

SMART’s foundational belief is that access to credible information is central to justice, economic opportunity, and social mobility. It works to reduce information asymmetry by using traditional and emerging communication technologies to support informed decision-making within communities. Its mission has consistently centred on poverty reduction through gender equity, improved family health, environmental protection, and the strengthening of democratic participation, particularly among minorities and marginalised groups.

Radio Mewat

SMART runs and is licensed to operate Radio Mewat, a community radio station based in Nuh (formerly Mewat), historically identified as one of India’s most socio-economically challenged districts.

Radio Mewat reaches over 165 villages and approximately 550,000 people through a 12-hour daily broadcast. The station is community-run, with local members serving as radio jockeys, producers, and contributors. This model has created ownership, credibility, and sustained trust within the region.

Programming focuses on rights awareness, government schemes, health, education, financial inclusion, and governance. Where broadcast reach is limited, SMART supplements radio transmission with narrowcasting and on-ground community engagement to ensure last-mile access to information.

Radio Mewat has received two National Awards for excellence in programming and sustainability. It has also received the SKOCH Order of Merit for its financial inclusion programming and has been featured in the Limca Book of Records for operating a high-engagement radio consumer helpline.

Institutional Evolution

While SMART’s early work focused on community awareness and empowerment, its institutional strategy has evolved toward building and strengthening the Community Media Ecosystem in India.

Today, SMART designs programme architectures, manages funding flows, builds implementation standards, publishes research, and convenes alliances of community media practitioners across radio, print, digital, and social media. It positions community media as civic infrastructure capable of addressing complex development challenges.
