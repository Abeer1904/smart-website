Strengthening India’s Community Media Ecosystem

SMART addresses the wicked problem of information asymmetry by working with hyperlocal community media practitioners to ensure that hard-to-reach communities receive essential, credible information in their own language — enabling better decisions and stronger futures.

Our Theory of Change

SMART addresses the problem of information asymmetry in hard-to-reach communities by working through community media practitioners to deliver essential, credible information in local languages.

When communities access trusted information through hyperlocal communicators, they are better equipped to make informed decisions, improve livelihoods, strengthen resilience, and shape their own futures.

What is Community Media?

Community media consists of locally anchored radio, print, digital, and social media practitioners who operate within and for specific communities. Unlike mass media, community media is participatory, accountable to local realities, and capable of building sustained dialogue rather than one-directional messaging.

How We Work

SMART builds structured programmes that move from design to activation through trusted local media institutions.

Programmes are developed centrally through research and thematic working groups, and implemented through independent community media organisations embedded within their communities. Strategy, capital, and accountability remain coherent, while delivery remains hyperlocal and trusted.

Community media functions as civic infrastructure — convening spaces where information, aspiration, and opportunity intersect.
