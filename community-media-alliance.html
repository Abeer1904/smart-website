Community Media Alliance (CMA)

The Community Media Alliance (CMA) is SMART’s vehicle for building a Global South community media ecosystem through segmentation, working groups, and fundable programme architectures. It brings together community media practitioners across radio, print, digital, and social media who work inside communities to provide credible information that enables people to make better decisions and build better futures.

What CMA is

Alliance purpose and operating model

CMA is an alliance of practitioners and institutions that treats community media as civic infrastructure, not as passive broadcasting. We organise the ecosystem into clear focus areas, place stations and practitioners within those focus areas, and build shared practice through working groups. Each working group produces a mini-manifesto, a programme blueprint, and a portfolio of participating stations and partners. This is how CMA becomes legible to funders, research institutions, and policy actors.

Why now

Why CMA exists

CMA is a response to a world shaped by an interconnected set of crises—often described as a polycrisis—where institutions struggle to maintain trust and communities struggle to access reliable information. Community media cannot remain a passive channel in this context. It must become an active, organised, and accountable layer of civic capacity: countering misinformation, enabling local problem-solving, and building pathways to livelihoods, dignity, and resilience. CMA’s intent is to shift hyperlocal media away from doom-driven attention cycles and towards progress-oriented storytelling that is grounded in community aspirations and measurable change.

How it works

Operating cycle

CMA runs through a repeatable operating cycle. SMART defines working groups and governance for shared practice. Each working group produces a mini-manifesto that sets the problem frame, the theory of change, and the ethical guardrails. SMART then clusters participating stations and practitioners into that working group, defines an implementation protocol (content formats, SOPs, QA, monitoring), and builds a partner-facing programme package. Programmes are executed through member organisations, and results are returned as comparable outputs, learning notes, and publishable knowledge products.

Fundable working groups and station clustering

Working groups are programme containers with a manifesto, an activation protocol, and a station cluster. Each one can be funded as a clear portfolio with defined outputs and research collaboration pathways.

It explicitly maps barriers to protein uptake through a gendered lens, and converts findings into story formats, community dialogues, and measurable behaviour pathways.

South–South solidarity and publishing

Convene, publish, and strengthen legitimacy

CMA exists to build South–South solidarity in community media practice. It will convene practitioners and researchers, organise talks and exchanges, and produce joint publications that strengthen the legitimacy of community media as a modern civic institution. CMA will also act as a publication engine: turning programme evidence into toolkits, manuals, and annual reporting—starting with a State of Community Media report that makes the sector visible to funders and policy audiences.

Partner value

What partners get

Partners get an organised entry point into a fragmented sector. They get segmentation by focus area, a pre-assembled station cluster, a tested activation protocol, and a reporting structure designed for comparability and learning. They also get the ability to run research collaborations ethically and efficiently, because CMA sets shared standards for consent, editorial practice, QA, and documentation.

Access

A credible gateway to distributed, trusted last-mile media organisations.

Comparability

Shared standards and reporting that enable aggregation across regions.
