Civic Games Lab

Civic Games Lab is an independent vertical within SMART. It develops games and simulation-based methods to reduce information asymmetry and support decision-making for complex social, governance, and climate challenges.

What it does

Capabilities

This page is structured as a vertical profile. The Lab is a tool that can be embedded into SMART programmes when games or scenario rehearsal improve outcomes.

Serious games

Designs participatory games to make complex systems legible and actionable.

Scenarios and simulations

Builds rehearsal spaces for strategy, governance, and decision-making under uncertainty.

Facilitation and training

Runs structured workshops for institutions, implementers, and community stakeholders.

Game-enabled monitoring

Creates feedback loops and learning artefacts to improve programme design and iteration.

Tools for implementation

Develops rulebooks, SOPs, and facilitation kits for partners to run sessions independently.

Research-to-design translation

Converts evidence and field insight into playable systems and decision aids.

How it plugs into SMART

Where the Lab strengthens programmes

Programme design phase

Use games to surface community constraints, map stakeholder incentives, and test intervention approaches before scale.

Activation and adoption

Use simulations and facilitated play to support behaviour shift, institutional alignment, and local decision making.
